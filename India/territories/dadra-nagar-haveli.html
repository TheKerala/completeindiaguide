Dadra & Nagar Haveli

A serene union territory, known for its lush greenery, tribal culture, and colonial heritage.

Quick Facts

  • Capital: Silvassa
  • Official Languages: Hindi, Gujarati, English
  • Formation: Became part of India in 1961
  • Nicknames: “Green Paradise”
  • Major Tribes: Warli, Dhodia, Kokna

History & Heritage

Dadra & Nagar Haveli was under Portuguese rule until 1954, after which it was integrated into India.

  • Colonial Era: Portuguese influence on culture and architecture.
  • Post-Independence: Merged with Daman & Diu for administrative purposes.

Geography & Climate

The region is characterized by lush forests, the Daman Ganga river, and hilly terrain.

  • Climate: Tropical with heavy monsoons and mild winters.

Top Places to Visit

  1. Vanganga Lake Garden: Scenic boating and gardens.
  2. Dudhni Lake: Water sports and tribal culture.
  3. Hirwa Van Gardens: Popular picnic spot.
  4. Tribal Museum: Insights into local culture.
  5. Khanvel: Serene landscape and wildlife.

Festivals

  • Tarpa Festival: Tribal dance and celebrations.
  • Diwali & Holi: Celebrated with vibrant customs.

Cuisine

The food blends Gujarati and tribal flavors.

  • Specials: Ubadiyu, Warli fish curry.
  • Sweets: Ghari, lapsi.

Arts & Culture

  • Warli paintings and tribal crafts.
  • Local folk music and dances.

Shopping & Handicrafts

  • Bamboo crafts and Warli art souvenirs.

How to Reach

  • By Air: Closest airport is Mumbai (140 km).
  • By Rail: Nearest station is Vapi (20 km).
  • By Road: Well connected to Mumbai and Gujarat.

Best Time to Visit

October to March is ideal for exploring the area.

Sample Itineraries

2 Days – Silvassa & Dudhni

  • Day 1: Silvassa – Tribal Museum, Hirwa Van Garden.
  • Day 2: Dudhni Lake – water activities and local culture.
