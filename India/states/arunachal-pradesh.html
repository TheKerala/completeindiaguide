Arunachal Pradesh

The “Land of the Rising Sun” — a mystical blend of Himalayan landscapes, tribal cultures, monasteries, wildlife, and adventure.

Quick Facts

  • Capital: Itanagar
  • Largest City: Itanagar
  • Official Languages: English (official), with 26 major tribal languages
  • Formation: 20 February 1987 (Statehood)
  • Nicknames: “Land of the Rising Sun”
  • Borders: Bhutan, China (Tibet), Myanmar
  • UNESCO Sites: None (Tawang Monastery is a cultural gem)

History & Heritage

Arunachal Pradesh, once known as the North-East Frontier Agency (NEFA), has deep ties to Tibetan Buddhism, tribal kingdoms, and Ahom influence. It became a union territory in 1972 and a state in 1987.

  • Tawang: Spiritual hub with the second-largest monastery in the world.
  • Tribal Heritage: 26 major tribes with vibrant festivals and customs.
  • Modern Era: Statehood achieved on 20 February 1987.

Geography & Climate

Arunachal Pradesh lies in the Eastern Himalayas, boasting lush forests, snow-clad peaks, and rich biodiversity.

  • Rivers: Siang, Subansiri, Lohit, Kameng, Tirap.
  • Climate: Alpine in highlands, subtropical in valleys; monsoon is intense.

Top Places to Visit (Handpicked)

  1. Tawang: Monastery, Sela Pass, Bum La Pass.
  2. Ziro Valley: Apatani villages, Ziro Music Festival.
  3. Bomdila: Monastery and panoramic views.
  4. Namdapha National Park: Wildlife paradise.
  5. Itanagar: Ita Fort, Ganga Lake.
  6. Roing & Mayudia: Lakes, wildlife sanctuaries.
  7. Pasighat: River rafting, eco-tourism.

Major Monasteries & Spiritual Circuits

  • Tawang Monastery: Largest in India.
  • Urgelling Monastery: Birthplace of 6th Dalai Lama.
  • Bomdila Monastery: Serene Buddhist site.

Festivals of Arunachal Pradesh

  • Losar: Tibetan New Year.
  • Nyokum: Nyishi tribe festival.
  • Solung: Adi tribe’s harvest celebration.
  • Reh: Idu Mishmi festival.
  • Ziro Music Festival: Popular music event.

Cuisine – What to Eat

Tribal and Himalayan flavors dominate the cuisine.

  • Staples: Rice, bamboo shoot dishes.
  • Specials: Thukpa, momos, apong (rice beer).

Arts, Handicrafts & Culture

  • Weaving by Apatani and Monpa tribes.
  • Cane & bamboo crafts.
  • Folk dances like Aji Lhamu.

Adventure & Wildlife

  • Trekking to Mechuka & Sela Pass.
  • Rafting on Siang.
  • Wildlife spotting in Namdapha.

How to Reach

  • By Air: Donyi Polo Airport (Itanagar).
  • By Rail: Naharlagun station.
  • By Road: Via Assam highways.

Best Time to Visit

October to April is ideal for tourism.

Famous Personalities

  • Pema Khandu: Chief Minister.
  • Talem Gao: Renowned musician.
