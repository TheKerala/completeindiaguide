Punjab

The “Land of Five Rivers” — rich in culture, heritage, spirituality, and cuisine.

Quick Facts

  • Capital: Chandigarh (shared with Haryana)
  • Largest City: Ludhiana
  • Official Language: Punjabi
  • Formation: 1 November 1966
  • Nicknames: “Granary of India”
  • Borders: Haryana, Himachal Pradesh, Jammu & Kashmir, Rajasthan
  • UNESCO Sites: None (Golden Temple is globally renowned)

History & Heritage

Punjab has a vibrant history, from the Indus Valley Civilization to Sikhism and India’s independence struggle.

  • Indus Valley Civilization: Harappa & Mohenjo-daro roots.
  • Sikh Heritage: Birthplace of Sikhism, home to Golden Temple.
  • Partition: Punjab was divided in 1947.

Geography & Climate

Punjab is a fertile plain, fed by five major rivers.

  • Rivers: Beas, Ravi, Sutlej, Chenab, Jhelum.
  • Climate: Extreme summers and winters; mild monsoon.

Top Places to Visit (Handpicked)

  1. Amritsar: Golden Temple, Jallianwala Bagh.
  2. Wagah Border: Beating Retreat ceremony.
  3. Anandpur Sahib: Historic Sikh town.
  4. Chandigarh: Rock Garden, Sukhna Lake.
  5. Patiala: Qila Mubarak, heritage sites.
  6. Ludhiana: Rural museums, textile industry.

Festivals of Punjab

  • Baisakhi: Harvest festival and Sikh New Year.
  • Lohri: Bonfires and cultural celebrations.
  • Gurpurab: Birth anniversaries of Sikh Gurus.
  • Hola Mohalla: Sikh martial arts festival.

Cuisine – What to Eat

Punjab’s cuisine is hearty, rich, and world-famous.

  • Staples: Makki di roti, sarson da saag.
  • Dishes: Butter chicken, Amritsari kulcha.
  • Sweets: Phirni, pinni, lassi.

Arts, Handicrafts & Culture

  • Phulkari embroidery and juttis.
  • Bhangra and Giddha dance forms.
  • Sikh religious music and heritage.

Adventure & Wildlife

  • Wildlife at Harike Wetland and Abohar Sanctuary.
  • Rural farm stays and tractor rides.
  • Heritage walks in Amritsar.

How to Reach

  • By Air: Sri Guru Ram Dass Jee International Airport (Amritsar).
  • By Rail: Amritsar, Ludhiana, Chandigarh stations.
  • By Road: Excellent highways and bus connectivity.

Best Time to Visit

October to March is ideal for a pleasant trip to Punjab.

Sample Itineraries

3 Days – Amritsar & Wagah

  • Day 1: Golden Temple, Jallianwala Bagh.
  • Day 2: Wagah Border ceremony.
  • Day 3: Local food and heritage walk.

4 Days – Heritage Punjab

  • Day 1: Amritsar.
  • Day 2: Anandpur Sahib.
  • Day 3: Patiala.
  • Day 4: Chandigarh sights.

Famous Personalities

  • Maharaja Ranjit Singh: The Lion of Punjab.
  • Baba Deep Singh: Sikh warrior saint.
