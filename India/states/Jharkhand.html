Jharkhand

The “Land of Forests” — Jharkhand is known for its waterfalls, forests, tribal culture, and industrial towns.

Quick Facts

  • Capital: Ranchi
  • Largest City: Jamshedpur
  • Official Language: Hindi
  • Formation: 15 November 2000
  • Nicknames: “Land of Forests”
  • Borders: Bihar, Uttar Pradesh, Chhattisgarh, Odisha, West Bengal
  • UNESCO Sites: None

History & Heritage

Jharkhand has a rich tribal heritage and was once a part of Bihar before becoming a separate state in 2000.

  • Tribal Legacy: Home to Santhal, Munda, and Ho tribes.
  • Colonial Era: Known for freedom fighters like Birsa Munda.
  • Modern Formation: Statehood achieved on 15 November 2000.

Geography & Climate

The state is rich in forests, hills, and mineral resources.

  • Major Rivers: Damodar, Subarnarekha, Koel.
  • Climate: Tropical with hot summers and cool winters.

Top Places to Visit (Handpicked)

  1. Ranchi: Rock Garden, Dassam Falls.
  2. Netarhat: Known as the ‘Queen of Chotanagpur’.
  3. Deoghar: Baidyanath Jyotirlinga temple.
  4. Jamshedpur: Tata Steel city and Jubilee Park.
  5. Betla National Park: Wildlife haven.
  6. Hazaribagh: Hill retreat and wildlife sanctuary.

Festivals of Jharkhand

  • Sarhul: Tribal spring festival.
  • Karma: Tribal harvest festival.
  • Chhath Puja: Major sun festival.

Cuisine – What to Eat

Jharkhand cuisine is simple yet flavorful.

  • Staples: Rice, dal, vegetables.
  • Specials: Litti Chokha, Dhuska, Thekua.

Arts, Handicrafts & Culture

  • Tribal art forms like Sohrai and Kohbar paintings.
  • Bamboo and wood crafts.
  • Traditional tribal dances.

Adventure & Wildlife

  • Trekking in Netarhat hills.
  • Wildlife safari in Betla National Park.
  • Exploring waterfalls like Hundru Falls.

How to Reach

  • By Air: Birsa Munda Airport (Ranchi).
  • By Rail: Ranchi, Jamshedpur, and Dhanbad stations.
  • By Road: Well-connected via highways.

Best Time to Visit

October to March is ideal for exploring the state.

Sample Itineraries

3 Days – Ranchi & Waterfalls

  • Day 1: Ranchi city tour.
  • Day 2: Dassam & Hundru Falls.
  • Day 3: Rock Garden & return.

4 Days – Deoghar & Hazaribagh

  • Day 1: Ranchi → Deoghar.
  • Day 2: Baidyanath temple & nearby spots.
  • Day 3: Hazaribagh wildlife sanctuary.
  • Day 4: Return.

Famous Personalities

  • Birsa Munda: Tribal freedom fighter.
  • Mahendra Singh Dhoni: Former Indian cricket captain.
