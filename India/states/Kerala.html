Kerala

Known as “God’s Own Country,” Kerala is a tropical paradise famous for its backwaters, beaches, hill stations, and Ayurveda.

Quick Facts

  • Capital: Thiruvananthapuram
  • Largest City: Kochi
  • Official Language: Malayalam
  • Formation: 1 November 1956
  • Nicknames: “God’s Own Country”
  • Borders: Karnataka, Tamil Nadu, Arabian Sea
  • UNESCO Sites: Western Ghats (part)

History & Heritage

Kerala’s history is shaped by trade, colonial influences, and rich cultural traditions. It was an important spice trade center attracting Arabs, Chinese, Portuguese, Dutch, and British traders.

  • Chera Dynasty: Ancient rulers of Kerala.
  • Colonial Era: Portuguese, Dutch, and British influences.
  • State Formation: Merged as Kerala in 1956.

Geography & Climate

Kerala lies on the Malabar Coast with lush backwaters, beaches, and the Western Ghats.

  • Rivers: Periyar, Bharathapuzha, Pamba.
  • Climate: Tropical monsoon with heavy rains.

Top Places to Visit (Handpicked)

  1. Alleppey: Backwaters and houseboats.
  2. Munnar: Tea gardens and hill stations.
  3. Kochi: Fort Kochi and Chinese fishing nets.
  4. Kumarakom: Bird sanctuary and backwaters.
  5. Thekkady: Periyar wildlife sanctuary.
  6. Kovalam: Famous beach destination.

Festivals of Kerala

  • Onam: Harvest festival with boat races.
  • Vishu: Traditional New Year.
  • Thrissur Pooram: Grand temple festival.

Cuisine – What to Eat

Kerala cuisine is known for coconut-based dishes, seafood, and spices.

  • Staples: Rice, coconut, curry leaves.
  • Specials: Appam with stew, Kerala Sadya, Puttu, Karimeen pollichathu.

Arts, Handicrafts & Culture

  • Kathakali and Mohiniyattam dance forms.
  • Coir and brass handicrafts.
  • Ayurveda and traditional wellness practices.

Adventure & Wildlife

  • Houseboat cruises in backwaters.
  • Trekking in Munnar and Wayanad.
  • Wildlife spotting in Periyar and Silent Valley.

How to Reach

  • By Air: Airports at Kochi, Trivandrum, and Calicut.
  • By Rail: Extensive rail connectivity across the state.
  • By Road: NH66 and state highways connect major cities.

Best Time to Visit

September to March is ideal for sightseeing and houseboat experiences.

Sample Itineraries

4 Days – Backwaters & Beaches

  • Day 1: Kochi sightseeing.
  • Day 2: Houseboat in Alleppey.
  • Day 3: Kumarakom and bird sanctuary.
  • Day 4: Kovalam beach.

5 Days – Hill Stations & Wildlife

  • Day 1: Kochi → Munnar.
  • Day 2: Explore tea gardens.
  • Day 3: Munnar → Thekkady.
  • Day 4: Periyar wildlife safari.
  • Day 5: Return via Kochi.

Famous Personalities

  • Sree Narayana Guru: Spiritual leader.
  • Arundhati Roy: Booker Prize-winning author.
  • Mohanlal: Legendary Malayalam actor.
