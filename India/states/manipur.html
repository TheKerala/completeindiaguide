Manipur

The “Jewelled Land” of India — famous for its classical dance, lush valleys, and vibrant tribal culture.

Quick Facts

  • Capital: Imphal
  • Largest City: Imphal
  • Official Language: Meitei (Manipuri)
  • Formation: 21 January 1972
  • Nicknames: “Jewelled Land of India”
  • Borders: Nagaland, Assam, Mizoram, Myanmar
  • UNESCO Sites: None (Loktak Lake is iconic)

History & Heritage

Manipur has a long history of independent kingdoms, rich tribal heritage, and classical dance forms. It merged with India in 1949 and became a state in 1972.

  • Kangla Fort: Symbol of Meitei heritage.
  • Vaishnav Influence: Flourishing culture of Manipuri dance and arts.
  • Modern Era: Statehood on 21 January 1972.

Geography & Climate

Manipur is a hill state with lush valleys and scenic lakes.

  • Main Lake: Loktak Lake (famous for floating phumdis).
  • Climate: Subtropical; cool winters and rainy summers.

Top Places to Visit (Handpicked)

  1. Loktak Lake: Floating islands and boating.
  2. Kangla Fort: Historical landmark of Imphal.
  3. Keibul Lamjao National Park: Home to Sangai deer.
  4. Imphal: Cultural hub and market exploration.
  5. Ukhrul: Hills, waterfalls, and trekking.
  6. Andro Village: Pottery and tribal life.
  7. Moreh: Indo-Myanmar border town.

Festivals of Manipur

  • Yaoshang: Holi of Manipur.
  • Ningol Chakouba: Family feast tradition.
  • Kut: Harvest festival of Kuki-Chin tribes.
  • Cheiraoba: Manipuri New Year.

Cuisine – What to Eat

Manipur’s cuisine is healthy, aromatic, and rich in local herbs.

  • Staples: Rice, fish, and seasonal vegetables.
  • Specials: Eromba, Chamthong (stew), Nga-thongba (fish curry).

Arts, Handicrafts & Culture

  • Classical Manipuri dance and Ras Leela.
  • Bamboo crafts and textiles.
  • Tribal weaving traditions.

Adventure & Wildlife

  • Trekking in Ukhrul and Dzuko Valley.
  • Boating on Loktak Lake.
  • Wildlife spotting in Keibul Lamjao.

How to Reach

  • By Air: Imphal International Airport.
  • By Rail: Nearest station is Dimapur (Nagaland).
  • By Road: Connected via highways from Assam and Nagaland.

Best Time to Visit

October to March is ideal for pleasant weather and festivals.

Sample Itineraries

3 Days – Imphal

  • Day 1: Loktak Lake & Keibul Lamjao.
  • Day 2: Kangla Fort & Ima Keithel (women’s market).
  • Day 3: Andro Village exploration.

5 Days – Hills & Valleys

  • Day 1: Imphal sightseeing.
  • Day 2: Ukhrul trek.
  • Day 3: Loktak Lake activities.
  • Day 4: Moreh border town visit.
  • Day 5: Return via cultural markets.

Famous Personalities

  • Mary Kom: Olympic boxer and world champion.
  • Ras Lila Artists: Renowned for classical dance.
